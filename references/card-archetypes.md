# Card Archetypes

Choose one card type per card. The source picks the type, not the other way around.
Each type has a job, a layout, and a Chinese text example.

## 金句卡 / Quote

Use when: the source is a quote, a line, an aphorism, a one-sentence opinion worth keeping.

Layout: the quote is dominant and centered, filling the calm middle of the card. Attribution
sits small and quiet below. Lots of negative space around the line. Optional one large faint
quotation mark behind or beside the text.

Text example:
> 你以为的稳定，
> 只是别人懒得换掉你。
>
> —— 某条让人安静的留言

## 干货清单卡 / Tip List

Use when: the source is a list of tips, a checklist, a "N 个…", a toolkit.

Layout: a title at the top, then 3–7 short numbered points in a clean left-aligned column.
Numbers are a quiet accent; each point is ONE short line. Clear hierarchy between the title
and the points — NOT equal-weight PPT bullets where everything is the same size.

Text example:
> 写出不尬文案的 5 条
> 1  先写给一个人，不是所有人
> 2  砍掉第一句，它通常是废话
> 3  把形容词换成事实
> 4  读一遍，删掉一半的「的」
> 5  结尾给一个动作，不要总结

## 读书笔记卡 / Book Note

Use when: the source is a book takeaway, a passage, a reading note.

Layout: a small header with book title + author at the top. One key takeaway as the hero in
the middle. Optional one highlighted line below (a short quote from the book) on a subtle
accent block. Everything else stays quiet.

Text example:
> 《被讨厌的勇气》· 岸见一郎
>
> 所有烦恼，
> 都是人际关系的烦恼。
>
> 「自由，就是被别人讨厌。」

## 数据卡 / Data

Use when: the source is a statistic, a number, a single striking figure.

Layout: one big number is the hero — large, bold, the first thing the eye hits. A short
context label sits above or below it explaining what the number means. A tiny source line
sits at the very bottom. One accent color on the number only.

Text example:
> 每天多睡 1 小时
>
> 23%
>
> 决策失误率下降
> 来源：某睡眠研究综述

## 步骤卡 / Steps

Use when: the source is a how-to, a process, a sequence.

Layout: numbered steps in a clean vertical flow, top to bottom. Each step is one verb-led
line. The numbers form a quiet rail down the left. A small title at the top names the goal.
Even spacing between steps so the flow reads as a path.

Text example:
> 3 步搞定朋友圈排版
> ① 一句话一行，别挤成一段
> ② 重点放最前，别埋在中间
> ③ 结尾空一行，留白比凑字耐看

## 概念卡 / Concept

Use when: the source is a term, a definition, a piece of jargon worth explaining.

Layout: the term is the hero at the top or center. Below it, a one-line plain-language
definition in supporting weight. Below that, a tiny concrete example. Three layers, clearly
sized apart.

Text example:
> 沉没成本
>
> 已经花出去、收不回来的钱或时间。
>
> 例：电影难看，但因为票买了就硬看完。

## Choosing Notes

- A quote or single strong line → 金句卡.
- A list of tips or a toolkit → 干货清单卡.
- A book takeaway → 读书笔记卡.
- A striking number → 数据卡.
- A process or how-to → 步骤卡.
- A term to explain → 概念卡.

One idea per card. Never stack two types — a list crammed under a quote, or a number bolted
onto a definition, is how cards lose their focal point. Pick the type that matches the source,
and let everything else stay quiet around the one thing the card carries.

# Quote Cards Style DNA

Use this reference before final card generation.

Quote Cards makes typographic cards whose identity comes from typographic discipline:
one dominant text element, clear hierarchy, generous negative space, and restraint.
The card reads as something a person would screenshot and save — not a PPT slide,
not a poster, not a 微商 image.

## Core Look

- Typographic-led: the words and their arrangement are the whole image. No big imagery.
- Default square 1:1 (1080×1080). Use 3:4 vertical (1080×1440) for longer cards — lists,
  steps, book notes that need more lines.
- One dominant text element — the hero line or number — with strong scale contrast against
  everything else on the card.
- Clear hierarchy: the eye lands on the hero first, then the supporting layer, then the
  meta layer (source, page, author).
- Generous, consistent margins. The card breathes. Negative space is a feature, not waste.
- Margins: keep ≥ 8% of the card edge clear on all four sides as empty ground (≈ 86px on a 1080
  card); if the hero touches inside that band, it is crammed — regenerate.
- Paper / ink base: an off-white or soft tinted ground, a deep ink for text. At most one
  accent color. Optional subtle paper grain or a single accent block.
- Max two fonts. One alignment, held throughout.
- Quiet decoration only: a thin rule, a large quotation mark, a small accent block, a number.
- Correct, clean Chinese characters — no melted, doubled, or gibberish glyphs.

## Hierarchy Rules

The hierarchy carries the card. It must:

- have exactly one hero — the line, quote, number, or term the card exists for
- make the hero clearly the largest / boldest thing, not just slightly bigger
- Scale: the hero's cap-height must be ≥ 2× the support's. If you cannot tell at a glance which
  line is the hero, the contrast failed.
- give the supporting layer a quieter weight and a smaller size
- shrink the meta layer (源 / 页码 / 作者 / 来源) to a small, calm footnote
- never make everything the same size — that is the PPT trap
- let the eye move 1 → 2 → 3 without confusion

The type does the work. The decoration stays quiet.

## Not a PPT Slide

Do not make the card look like a slide:

- no equal-weight bullets where every point is the same size and color
- no title bar / colored header strip slapped across the top of everything
- no full-width footer band, no slide number, no logo lockup in the corner
- no four quadrants of boxed content competing for attention
- no "标题 + 正文 + 要点 1/2/3" template with no focal line

## Not a Poster

Do not let imagery take over:

- no large photo, illustration, or background scene dominating the text
- no decorative figure, mascot, or character — this is a text card
- no gradient sky, no scenic backdrop, no product hero shot
- the card is words and space, with at most one tiny graphic accent

## Not 微商 / Cluttered

Do not make the card look like spam or a crammed flyer:

- no gold/red "暴富" glow, sparkle bursts, or urgency badges
- no scattered emoji, sticker pile, or QR-code cram
- no rainbow palette, no six competing colors, no gradient glow on text
- no edge-to-edge text with no margins; no five ideas fighting for the frame
- no warped WordArt, bevel, double drop shadow, or neon-on-neon

## Not AI Plastic

Reject the tells of a careless AI image:

- gibberish text, doubled characters, or fake glyphs posing as Chinese
- melted, smeared, or half-formed letters in the hero line
- hallucinated extra text, watermark, or logo
- mushy, plastic-looking decorative elements

## World-Class Quality Bar

Accept only if:

- there is one savable idea and the hero is clearly the largest element
- the eye lands on the hero first, then support, then meta — hierarchy is unmistakable
- margins are generous and the composition has breathing room, not over-full
- the palette is restrained (≤ 1 accent, ≤ 2 fonts) and the characters are correct
- it feels worth screenshotting and keeping

Reject if:

- everything is the same weight and there is no focal line
- it reads as a PPT slide, a poster, or a 微商 image
- it is cluttered, over-full, or carries more than one idea
- the palette is a rainbow or the text is garbled
- more than 2 fonts OR more than 1 accent color beyond paper+ink — count them; if the count
  exceeds the budget, regenerate
- it is neat but says nothing worth saving
